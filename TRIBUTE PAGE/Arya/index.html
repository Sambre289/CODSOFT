★ Father of the Indian Nuclear Programme ★

☘ Jehangir Bhabha (1909–1966) was an Indian nuclear physicist who is considered the father of the Indian nuclear program. He played a crucial role in the development of nuclear science and technology in India. Here is a detailed overview of his life and contributions:

☘ Early Life and Education :
→ Birth: Born on October 30, 1909, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, into a wealthy Parsi family.
→ Education: Attended Elphinstone College and the Royal Institute of Science, Bombay, before moving to England.
→ Higher Education: Studied at the University of Cambridge, where he earned a degree in Mechanical Engineering in 1930 and subsequently pursued a doctorate in theoretical physics under the supervision of Paul Dirac.

☘ Bhabha Scientific Contributions :
→ Cosmic Ray Research: Bhabha is renowned for his work on cosmic rays. He conducted groundbreaking research that led to the understanding of the properties of these high-energy particles.
s → Bhabha Scattering: One of his significant contributions is the theoretical explanation of electron-positron scattering, which is now known as Bhabha scattering.

☘ Career and Achievements :
→ Return to India: In 1939, he returned to India due to the outbreak of World War II. He joined the Indian Institute of Science in Bangalore, where he worked with Nobel laureate C.V. Raman.
→ Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR): In 1945, he founded TIFR in Bombay, which became the center of nuclear research in India. → Atomic Energy Commission: Appointed as the first chairman of the Indian Atomic Energy Commission in 1948. He played a pivotal role informulating India’s nuclear policy and spearheading its atomic energy program.

☘ International Contributions :
→ International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA): Bhabha was instrumental in the formation of the IAEA and was elected as the President of the United Nations Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy in 1955.
→ Awards and Honors: He received several prestigious awards, including the Padma Bhushan in 1954. He was also elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society

☘ Legacy :
→ Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC): In his honor, the Atomic Energy Establishment, Trombay was renamed as Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) after his death.
→ Inspiration: Bhabha’s vision and contributions have left a lasting impact on India’s scientific community and its nuclear energy program, paving the way for advancements in science and technology in the country.
